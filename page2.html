Facts About Risks and Effects

Among Kids and Teens

Academic

 1) Frequent or long-term marijuana graduation use is linked to school
dropout and lower educational achievement

2)Students who smoke marijuana may get lower grades and may more likely to drop out of high school than their peers who do not use

Health Effects

1) Research shows that marijuana use can have permanent effects on brain function in the developing brain when use begins in adolescence, especially with regular or heavy use

2) Marijuana use has been linked to a range of mental health problems in teens such as depression or anxiety

3) Research shows that about 1 in 6 teens who repeatedly use marijuana can become addicted, which means that they may make unsuccessful efforts to quit using marijuana or may give up important activities with friends and family in favor of using marijuana

Teen Facts

1) 38% of high school students report having used marijuana in their life

2) Statistics show that about 7 percent, or roughly 1 in 14 teens, report using marijuana in the past month

Peer Pressure

1) Peer Pressure can result in Early Abuse of drugs or alcohol use

2) Teens under peer pressure may experience loss of self-control

3)  70% of teens that smoke have friends who smoke or were peer pressured to smoke

4)" Teens who do drugs and drink are very likely to peer pressure others to do so
